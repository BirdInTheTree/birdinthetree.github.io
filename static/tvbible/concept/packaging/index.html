Packaging и путь к байеру

levyhaugelandau

Суть

Packaging — это искусство сборки творческих элементов проекта: идея + автор + продюсер + потенциально режиссёр и звезда [1]. Правильный package усиливает проект; неправильный элемент может его утопить [2]. Агент выполняет ключевую функцию — связывает авторов с правильными байерами и знает потребности каждой сети [3]. Без агентского представительства нельзя питчить сетям — это защищает и юридически [4]. Referral — самый эффективный способ попасть к байеру [5]. Целить стоит не в звёздных продюсеров, а в development executives — людей, чья работа — находить материал [6]. Продюсеры работают на development бесплатно — они получают деньги только когда проект идёт в производство [7]. Авторский гонорар за пилотный сценарий — от $50K до $750K [8].

Цитаты

[1] "The process of assembling the various creative elements of development is known as 'packaging.' Putting projects together, marrying an idea with a writer, potentially partnering them with a producer, possibly adding a director and/or a star." — Levy, p.18

[2] "Packaging is one of the fundamental arts of development. The goal is to marry creative elements that harmonize, that work together and off each other to make the best possible version of the project. Packaging the wrong creative element can sink a project." — Levy, p.18

[3] "The most important function of a literary agent in TV development is to connect writers with the right buyers. Agents know the development strategies, tastes and current needs of every network." — Levy, p.178

[4] "Writers should never pitch or submit material to networks without agent representation. Beyond the practical benefits, the legal protections are essential." — Levy, p.183

[5] "A referral is the single most effective way to get your material read. If someone in the business is willing to recommend your work, the buyer is far more likely to be receptive." — Hauge, p.77

[6] "Don't target star-level producers or A-list directors. Target the development executives. These are the people whose job it is to find material." — Hauge, p.73

[7] "Interestingly, producers don't get paid for development. Producers get paid when and if the development phase is successful and the project moves forward to pilot and/or series production." — Levy, p.17

[8] "For most TV development, networks and studios together pay anywhere from $50,000 to $750,000 for a writer to write one 35-60-page pilot script." — Levy, p.17

[9] "Don't pursue the major studios directly. Instead, focus on production companies that have deals with the studios. These are the companies actively looking for material." — Hauge, p.74

[10] "If you're trying to get in the door to pitch your original series to a network, the trick is to get producers with a proven track record to read your very best work." — Landau, p.319

[11] "Seek out TV production companies that are actively developing series for the new pitching season. The producers to target are ones with Production Overall Deals (known as PODS) at studios and networks." — Landau, p.318

[12] "Agency packaging is the practice of a talent agency assembling multiple client elements on a single project -- writer, director, actors -- and taking a packaging fee rather than individual commissions." — Levy, p.181
