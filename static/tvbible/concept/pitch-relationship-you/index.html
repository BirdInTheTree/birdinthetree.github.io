Автор как личность: внутренняя игра

harrislevylandaunash

Суть

Байеры покупают не только идею — они инвестируют в автора [1]. Personal way in — личная связь с материалом — ломает лёд и демонстрирует, что автор — именно тот человек, который должен написать этот сериал [2]. Отказ — это нормальная часть процесса, а не оценка таланта; всё когда-то отвергалось [3][4]. Критика — знак того, что в авторе видят потенциал; никто не тратит время на безнадёжных [5]. Прокрастинация часто маскирует страх отказа [6]. «Secondary gain» — скрытая выгода от бездействия — главный враг продуктивности [7]. Ключ к устойчивости — ясные ценности и конкретные, измеримые цели вместо размытых [8][9]. «Take» — уникальный авторский взгляд на концепцию — то, что делает проект неповторимым [10]. Landau оспаривает аксиому «write what you know» — незнание можно исследовать [11].

Цитаты

[1] "Even the most solitary screenwriter will eventually and inevitably end up in a conference room or executive's office to tell them the story. The potential buyers are not simply buying a well-executed, original idea. They're investing in you." — Landau, p.313

[2] "The Personal Way In breaks the ice, helps the writer relax, demonstrates to the buyer that the writer is the right person to write this particular show, and introduces the themes of the series in a personal way." — Levy, p.101

[3] "Being rejected comes with the job - it's nothing to be ashamed of. Sometimes it's simply a matter of timing." — Harris, p.129

[4] "It's all about timing. Everything I've done has been rejected some time." — Harris, p.130

[5] "Nobody wastes time criticising someone who has no future. Their intensity, albeit painful, is a sign that they feel you have potential." — Harris, p.131

[6] "A common reason for procrastination is that we are afraid of rejection. Disliking rejection is natural. However, the writer who never got rejected never sent anything out." — Harris, p.162

[7] "In the psychological studies of NLP this is called Secondary Gain. Something you are gaining by not doing what you really want to do." — Harris, p.164

[8] "The clearer you are about your own reasons and values, the stronger will become your desire to do the work and overcome the many obstacles that stand in your way." — Harris, p.163

[9] "'Selling your script' is too vague. 'Sending out one query letter a week' is far more likely to be achieved." — Harris, p.165

[10] "A take is the specific creative approach to a concept area that makes the concept feel new and different. The take is the writer's unique angle, the creative reinvention that distinguishes the new concept from similar shows that came before." — Levy, p.75

[11] "I do not believe in the writer's axiom (or cliché): Write what you know. It certainly helps to write about what you know better than anyone else, but we writers are curious and inquisitive creatures. What you don't know, you can research!" — Landau, p.314

[12] "Rejection doesn't mean you're bad. It means you were brave enough to finish a script and brave enough to make an approach to a possible buyer." — Harris, p.162

[13] "Just as your characters have an inner story, so do you. You, too, have to overcome your flaws if you are to achieve your happy ending." — Harris, p.161

[14] "Until you sign a contract, you're in charge of your script. Up till then, you don't have to do what anyone says." — Harris, p.132
