Writers' room

levylandau

Суть

Телевизионное письмо — это глубоко коллаборативный процесс; нужно быть открытым лучшей идее, когда она появится [1]. В writers' room действует принцип «the best idea wins» — неважно, от кого она пришла [2]. При этом автору необходимо владеть материалом: даже при адаптации чужого IP сценарист должен сделать историю своей [3]. Writer — тот, кто доставляет питч; в традиционной модели автор лично приходит к development executives и питчит идею [4].

Цитаты

[1] "Writing for television is highly collaborative. Stay open to the better idea if and when it arrives." — Landau, p.313

[2] "Lesli Linka Glatter, the producing-director EP of Homeland and the director of the Gilmore Girls and Pretty Little Liars pilots, refers to this as 'The best idea wins' approach. 'This is an art that we do with many people,' she says. 'I don't need to be the smartest person in the room. I want to be in the room with the smartest people.'" — Levy, p.278

[3] "What I learned as a producer at Alloy, focusing primarily on adaptation, was that TV writers need to have ownership of the ideas they're working on if they're going to imbue them with a genuinely creative and artistic spirit." — Levy, p.259

[4] "The writer is the person who delivers the pitch. In traditional pilot development, the writer walks into a conference room at a network and pitches the idea for a TV series to a roomful of network development executives." — Levy, p.99
