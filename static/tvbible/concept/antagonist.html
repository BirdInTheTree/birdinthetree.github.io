Антагонист

Персонажи
obergharrisnashlandaulevy

Суть

Антагонист — не обязательно злодей, а главная сила, противостоящая протагонисту [1][2]. В character-led историях протагонист сам является своим антагонистом, а функцию внешнего давления выполняет catalyst character [3]. Антагонист давит именно на flaw героя сильнее всех остальных персонажей [2]. Лучшие антагонисты сочетают положительные и отрицательные черты [7], а каждый злодей считает себя героем собственной истории [8]. При этом Harris подчёркивает, что без значимых stakes и нарастающих последствий провала антагонистическая сила теряет вес [5].

Цитаты

[1] "All villains are antagonists, but not all antagonists are villains." — Landau, p.195

[2] "The antagonist is the character who presses on the hero's flaw the hardest. They're not necessarily evil. They're just the biggest obstacle to the hero's need." — Nash, p.33

[3] "A catalyst character pushes, forces or helps the protagonist to change in a character-led story. They often look like an antagonistic character because they tend to oppose the protagonist's conscious goal. In fact, catalyst characters are co-protagonists on the protagonist's unconscious need to change." — Oberg, Glossary

[4] "Illustrate that drama is a struggle, not a statement. Does the pilot introduce a formidable antagonistic force, whether it's an external antagonist in a plot-led story, the protagonist themselves in a character-led story, or society in a theme-led story?" — Oberg, Chapter 3, Section 3.2, p.209

[5] "There must be consequences of failure - stakes. What happens if the protagonist doesn't achieve the goal? And those stakes need to escalate as the story progresses." — Harris, p.23

[6] "The antagonist will often have the same goal as the protagonist – but with a negative or evil agenda." — Landau, p.197

[7] "The most iconic antagonists possess both positive and negative characteristics." — Landau, p.198

[8] "Every villain is the hero of his/her/their own story." — Landau, p.199

[9] "Antagonists tend to be parasites who outwardly seek to get power from others... as opposed to protagonists, who tend to discover that their power lies within." — Landau, p.196

[10] "Antagonists almost always try to subvert the truth." — Landau, p.196

[11] "The antagonist will be the primary (usually negative) person or force that most actively challenges or threatens your protagonist." — Landau, p.195

[12] "Most villains do not change." — Landau, p.197

[13] "The goal must be difficult to achieve. There must be obstacles - and the biggest obstacle of all is usually another person: the antagonist." — Harris, p.22

[14] "Conflict is whatever stands in the way of the hero achieving her outer motivation. It is the sum total of all the obstacles, hurdles, and complications the hero faces in pursuit of that visible goal." — Hauge, p.35
